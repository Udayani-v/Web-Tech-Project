VEGETABLES DETAILS ( requirements )

Temperature:
Tomato:
Tomatoes need 3 to 
4 months of warm, 
clear, fairly 
dry weather to 
produce best. 


Cauliflower:
Cauliflower is a 
cool-season crop.


Chilli:
Chillies can be grown
in pots on a sunny, 
warm patio in a simi
lar way to tomatoes, 
but will produce a 
better crop when grown 
in a greenhouse.


Brinjal:
It is a warm season 
crop and is very 
susceptible to frost.
Rainfall:
Tomato:
It is grown in areas 
receiving an annual 
precipitation of 
5-10 inches.


Cauliflower:
It is grown in areas 
receiving an annual 
precipitation of 
25-30 inches.


Chilli:
It is grown in areas 
receiving an annual 
precipitation of 
25-30 inches.


Brinjal:
It is grown in areas 
receiving an annual 
precipitation of 
35-40 inches.
Soils:
Tomato:
Sandy loam soil 
with a well drained 
clay sub soil is 
best suited. 


Chilli:
Chilli can be grown 
in all type of soft 
but the sandy-loam, 
clay loam and loam 
soils are best 
suited for it. 


Cauliflower:
Soil should be 
rich in organic
matter.


Brinjal:
It is grown with 
considerable success 
in fine and rich 
loam soils that 
are deep and 
well-drained.
Surface:
Tomato:
The land is prepared 
by giving 2-3 plough
ings and clod crushi
ng after each plough
ing.


Cauliflower:
The land is prepared 
by giving 2-3 plough
ings and clod crushi
ng after each plough
ing.


Chilli:
The land is prepared 
by giving 2-3 plough
ings and clod crushi
ng after each plough
ing.


Brinjal:
The land is prepared 
by giving 2-3 plough
ings and clod crushi
ng after each plough
ing.
Fertilizers:
Vegetables requires 
three essential
plant nutrients: 
nitrogen, phosphorus
and potassium. 
Most paddy lands 
have a moderate 
quantity of such 
nutrients, but if 
they are deficient, 
organic manure or 
artificial fertilizers
have to be used.
Labour:
Vegetable cultivation 
is extremely 
labour-intensive,
therefore, requires
more labour in 
comparison to 
other cereal 
crops. Labour is 
necessary for: 
preparing the field, 
weeding, sowing, 
transplanting, manuring, 
harvesting, threshing, 
winnowing and milling.
For rice cultivation 
large number of cheap
labour is required.
